Devin Booker's take on Kendall Jenner-Bad Bunny rumors

Publish date: 2024-09-27

Devin Booker “finds it difficult to believe” that the rumored romance between his ex, Kendall Jenner, and rapper Bad Bunny can go the distance, according to a new report.

Speaking recently to Us Weekly, a source close to the Phoenix Suns guard weighed in on Jenner’s apparent relationship with the Puerto Rican musician, whom she has been linked to for months.

“Devin doesn’t believe for one minute that Kendall and Bad Bunny’s relationship is that serious,” the insider said.

“He doesn’t think he’s her type and finds it difficult to believe there’s any longevity there between them.”

Booker, 26, had dated Jenner on and off since 2020 before calling it quits in November.

Months later, the “Kardashians” star, 27, sparked dating rumors with Bad Bunny — whose real name is Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio — when they were seen dining at the same Beverly Hills restaurant in February.

Speculation over the nature of their relationship intensified the following month, when fans pondered if the 29-year-old rapper shaded Booker in the lyrics of a song.

In a verse featured on Eladio Carrión’s track “Coco Chanel,” Bad Bunny sang in Spanish, “I’m not bad baby, that’s a gimmick / But the Puerto Rico sun in warmer than the one in Phoenix.”

Although Jenner is clearly moving on, as she and Bad Bunny were seen together at Coachella over the weekend, the Booker insider suggested to Us Weekly that the NBA star “is not completely over her.”

“Devin still thinks about Kendall on occasion and a piece of him still misses her,” the source told the outlet.

“He has no idea what the future holds and although maybe their timing wasn’t right, doesn’t mean it can’t happen at some point down the line.”

The source also noted Booker is “not actively trying to get back with her but isn’t exactly opposed to the idea either.”

Prior to her relationship with Booker, whose Suns dropped Game 1 of their first-round NBA playoff series to the Clippers on Sunday, Jenner dated Brooklyn Nets point guard Ben Simmons.
