Longtime Judge Joanna Tabit dies aged 62 after battling cancer in Charleston

Publish date: 2024-09-24

Charleston, WV – The community of Charleston, West Virginia, is mourning the loss of esteemed Kanawha County Circuit Judge Joanna Tabit, who passed away at the age of 62 on September 29 after a courageous battle with cancer. Known for her unwavering dedication to justice and her compassionate approach, Tabit’s impact on the community is deeply felt and will forever be remembered.

Evan Osborne, executive director of Capital Market, expressed the sentiments of many, highlighting the significant void left by Judge Tabit’s passing. He emphasized the positive influence she had on Charleston and the lasting legacy she leaves behind, urging everyone to embrace kindness, a value she embodied throughout her life.

Judge Joanna Tabit, who served in her role as circuit judge since 2014, was described as fair, passionate, and respectful to all by those who had the privilege of knowing her. Ric Cavender, a Kanawha County Board of Education member, remembered her fondly as a friend and a beacon of light in the community. Tabit’s impact extended beyond the courtroom, leaving a mark of positivity wherever she went.

Charleston Mayor Amy Shuler Goodwin reflected on Judge Tabit’s commitment to public service and her genuine desire to see people succeed. She emphasized the need for the community to embody Tabit’s values, urging understanding, kindness, and fair treatment for all, mirroring the qualities she exuded.

To honor her memory and continue her impactful work, Tabit’s family plans to establish the Judge Santa Foundation, benefiting juvenile drug court. The foundation will carry forward Tabit’s dedication to helping young lives find the right path and contribute positively to society.

In this time of sorrow, the community unites in honoring Judge Joanna Tabit’s life and contributions, cherishing the legacy she leaves behind, and striving to emulate her values of fairness, kindness, and service to others.
