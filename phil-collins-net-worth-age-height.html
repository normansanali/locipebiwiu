Biography of Phil Collins

On January 30, 1951, Philip David Charles Collins was born in Chiswick, London, UK.

His mother, Winifred June Strange, worked as a theatrical agent, while his father, Greville Philip Austin Collins, was an insurance agent.

While his father had a routine job, Phil’s interest in work began when he was a little child. He would go to the theater with her to see plays and performances.

Collins started acting when he was a young child, and thanks to his mother’s connections with talent agencies, he was able to land his first theatrical roles.

He had a few minor roles in “Calamity the Cow” and “A Hard Day’s Night,” but acting had never truly satisfied him.

The rock group “Genesis” advertised for a drummer in the middle of the 1970s, and Collins joined the group in August 1970 after passing an audition. Collins performed on the band’s albums and toured with them for the following five years.

In 1981, he released his debut solo album, “Face Value,” which had a distinct sound that was more influenced by funk and faster beats. The album achieved widespread popularity, topping the charts in seven different nations.

Collins embarked on the successful “Turn It on Again: The Tour” tour in 2006 after reuniting with two former “Genesis” members.

Collins was given a spot in the “Rock & Roll Hall of Fame” in March 2010, and he announced his temporary retirement in 2011.
