Ernest Borgnine was an American actor who had a six-decade career. His voice was rough but calm, and he wore a gap-toothed Cheshire Cat grin.

He was a well-known entertainer who appeared as a guest on a number of talk shows and as a panelist on a number of game shows.

Borgnine’s cinematic career began in 1951 with supporting appearances in China Corsair, From Here to Eternity, Vera Cruz, Bad Day at Black Rock, and The Wild Bunch .

He has starred in a number of unorthodox films, receiving the Academy Award for Best Actor in 1956 for Marty (1955), which also won the Academy Award for Best Picture that year.

In addition to a wide range of other roles, Borgnine had continued success in the sitcom McHale’s Navy (1962–1966), in which he portrayed the titular character, and in the action series Airwolf (1984–1986), in which he co-starred as Dominic Santini.
