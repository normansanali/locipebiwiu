Isiah Thomas is an American former professional basketball player, philanthropist, and coach who works as an NBA TV analyst. The 12-time NBA All-Star was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame and named to the NBA’s 50th and 75th anniversary teams. He spent his entire NBA (National Basketball Association) career with the Detroit Pistons. He coached the Indiana Pacers, New York Knicks, and FIU from 2000 to 2012.

Who is Isiah Thomas?

Isiah Thomas III is Isiah Thomas II and Mary Thomas’s son. Thomas was born on April 30, 1961, in Chicago, Illinois, the youngest of nine children, and grew up on the city’s West Side. He began dribbling and shooting baskets as halftime entertainment at Catholic Youth Organisation games when he was three years old.

Isiah Thomas II, Thomas’s father, was a soldier in the Battle of Saipan. He later attended trade school and rose through the ranks to become the first black supervisor at International Harvester in Chicago. When the plant closed, the only job he could find was as a caretaker, and the family became impoverished. Isiah Thomas II abandoned the family when he was a child.

Isiah Thomas III commuted 90 minutes from his house to Our Lady of Sorrows School and St. Joseph High School in Westchester. Under coach Gene Pingatore, he led St. Joseph to the state championships in his junior year and was regarded as one of the country’s top college prospects.
