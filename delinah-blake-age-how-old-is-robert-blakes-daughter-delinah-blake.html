Who Was Robert Blake?

Robert Blake was an American actor known for appearing in the 1967 film In Cold Blood and the 1970s TV series Baretta.

Blake began his acting career by playing lead roles as a child actor in many films including the Red Ryder series, where he was portrayed as an American Indian and Latino character.

Blake worked briefly in the United States Army, but continued acting.

He is often referred to as “one of the longest in Hollywood history.”

During his lifetime, Blake had been accused of the 2001 murder of his second wife, Bonny Lee Bakley, and was found “liable for her wrongful death.”

Bakley was brutally shot in the head, outside a Los Angeles-area restaurant, while sitting in Blake’s car.
