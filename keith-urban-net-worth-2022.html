Keith Urban Early Life

Keith Lionel Urban was born on October 26, 1967, in Whangarei, New Zealand. His father had a convenience shop in Caboolture, Queensland, Australia, where he grew up. As a child, he began taking guitar lessons from his teacher, Sue McCarthy and entered local music competitions.

Urban debuted in the Australian talent competition “New Faces” in 1983. Throughout the rest of the 1980s, he maintained his presence in the Australian country music scene by appearing on television shows such as “The Reg Lindsay Country Homestead TV program” and “Mike McClellan’s Music Program,” as well as performing at venues such as the Northern Suburbs Country Music Club in Bald Hills.

Keith Urban Music Career

Urban signed a recording with EMI Australia in 1990. Between 1993 and 1994, he traveled as the backup act to Slim Dusty, releasing his self-titled debut album “Keith Urban.” His first visit to the Grand Ole Opry was as a support act for Dusty Springfield. Urban relocated to Nashville, Tennessee in 1992. Urban released his self-titled debut album in the United States in 1999. His single “Your Everything” reached No. 4 on the US country charts, marking him the first male New Zealand musician to do so. His first No. 1 hit came with the single “But for the Grace of God.” He won the Top New Male Vocalist Award at the 2001 Academy of Country Music Awards, as well as the Horizon Prize at the 2001 Country Music Association Awards.

In 2002, he released his second American album, “Golden Road.” Urban produced seven of the album’s thirteen songs on his own and co-produced the remaining six with Dann Huff.

The album’s first hit, “Somebody Like You,” spent six weeks at the top of the charts, and the fourth single, “You’ll Think of Me,” earned him a Grammy Award for Best Male Country Singing Style in 2005. “Golden Road” was certified triple platinum in September 2015, with 3 million copies sold. Urban made his first appearance in front of European audiences when supporting Bryan Adams on his UK and Ireland tour.

In 2004, he released the album “Be Here,” and the lead song “Days Go By” became his seventh No. 1 country hit. His 2006 single “Once in a Lifetime” debuted at No. 17, breaking the record for the highest-debuting country single at the time; it reached No. 6 at the time.

Keith Urban Appearances on Television

Urban became one of the four vocal coaches on the Australian edition of the reality singing competition “The Voice” in November 2011. After one season, he quit the show. Urban joined the judging panel for the twelfth season of “American Idol” in September 2012, according to the Fox Broadcasting Company. He was a judge alongside Randy Jackson, Mariah Carey, and Nicki Minaj, replacing Steven Tyler. Seasons 13, 14, and 15 saw him return.

Keith Urban Personal Life

Urban is a New Zealander, Australian, and American citizen. Urban met Australian actress Nicole Kidman at G’Day LA, a Hollywood event promoting Australia, in January 2005. Six months later, they started dating and married in June 2006. Sunday Rose (born in 2008) and Faith Margaret are their two kids (born in 2010).

Urban has a history of drug and alcohol problems. He used cocaine extensively while living in Nashville in the 1990s. In 1998, he checked himself into Cumberland Heights, a Nashville treatment facility. In 2006, he went to the Betty Ford Center in California for his second stint in recovery.

Urban initiated a lawsuit in 2007 against a New Jersey painter with the same name who held the website KeithUrban.com in order to obtain the domain name. The painter countersued, and the case was ultimately resolved in his favor.
