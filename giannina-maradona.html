Family & Ethnicity

Giannina Maradona belongs to a Roman Catholic family in Argentina. [1]

Parents & Siblings 

Giannina Maradona’s father, Diego Maradona, was a well-known Argentine soccer player. Her mother, Claudia Villafañe, is an Argentine businesswoman and producer, and her sister, Dalma Maradona, is an Argentine actress.

She has six half-siblings; five half-brothers, and a half-sister. Her half-sister, Jana Maradona, was born in 1996 and works as a lingerie model.

Her half-brother Diego Sinagra was born in 1986 as the result of an extra-marital affair between the legendary Argentine footballer Diego Maradona and Cristiana Sinagra, a young local woman and Italian model.

Her other half-brother Diego Fernando is from Diego Maradona’s relationship with Veronica Ojeda.

The names of her other half-siblings are Santiago Lara, Joana, Lu, and Javielito. [2]

Relationships, Husband & Children

In 2008, Giannina Maradona got married to Sergio Agüero, an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a striker for Premier League Club Manchester City, and the Argentine national team. In 2009, the couple had a son, Benjamín Agüero Maradona. In 2012, the couple eventually split up, following a divorce.

Career

Giannina Maradona works as an accessory designer for DeLaPaz, an online portal which sells exclusively designed jewelry and household decoratives in Buenos Aires, Argentina

Controversy

In 2018, Diego Maradona accused his wife, Claudia Villafañe, and his daughters, Dalma and Giannina, of stealing 3.4 million pounds from his bank account. The former soccer player claimed that his family transferred stolen cash to a bank account in Uruguay and also used his money to buy a property in the USA. Giannina responded to the allegation on Twitter, saying,

They know where I live and they can come when they want…Thanks for all the warm messages. I am fine with myself inside, I have forgiven worse from him and I will do the same now.’

Maradona accused his wife, Claudia Villafañe, of being the mastermind of this alleged fraud. [3]
