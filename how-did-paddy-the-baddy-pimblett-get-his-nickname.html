How Did Paddy &quot;The Baddy&quot; Pimblett get His Nickname?

Publish date: 2024-09-30

One of the most notable figures in the Ultimate Fighting Championship, is Paddy Pimblett. As he has risen to fame, fans have questioned the origin of his unique nickname "The Baddy" comes from. Join me on a journey to piece together just where this nickname came from and a look at Paddy Pimblett as a fighter, his history, and his substantial fighting record.


Who is Paddy Pimblett?

Born on January 3, 1995, in Liverpool, England, Paddy Pimblett is a professional MMA fighter. Capturing the eyes of fight fans with standout fights in the UFC Lightweight division since making his professional debut in 2012. For most of his fights, Jiu-Jitsu training is what has made him so successful and that has got him most of his wins through a mix of KO and submission.


THE BADDY Nickname

Fight fans have plenty of time to ponder the intriguing moniker "The Baddy". Somewhat surprisingly, this moniker comes from Pimblett's coach who gave him his unique title. What exactly lies behind this curious name choice remains unexplained, but it undoubtedly extends mystique and individuality to Pimblett and helps him stand out among mixed martial artists.


The Journey to Prominence

The rise of Pimblett to the highest levels of MMA has been a remarkable one. He began his professional career in 2012 at the age of 17 debuting with a take-no-prisoners, 3–0 record before signing with Cage Warriors. His career has had its peaks, with the first coming in 2016 when he won the Cage Warriors featherweight title, indicating his great ability early in his career. Pimblett has lived a career in the peaks and troughs of the MMA landscape, fighting throughout the divisions and grabbing some remarkable wins along the way as a fighter.


Statistical Brilliance

Looking at Pimblett’s fighting stats on paper, they tell you all that you need to know about how capable he is in the octagon. With a 24-21-3 (W-L-D) MMA record, he has as many wins by knockout as by submission, demonstrating his versatility and aptitude. Pimblett, who has arguably one of the most stellar track records in the lightweight division, compiled a three-round unanimous decision win over Tony Ferguson at UFC 296, and at UFC 282, he similarly defeated Jared Gordon.


A Fun and Analytical View

His meteoric trajectory, some stunning victories, and an incredibly exciting action style are still the resonating effects that kept drawing the attention of fans from all over the world. Paddy Pimblett shows what a fighter can achieve with determination and talent in the mixed martial arts world. And as he makes his mark on the UFC, the intrigue of his "The Baddy" moniker and the way he continues to win, will only grow his aura.
